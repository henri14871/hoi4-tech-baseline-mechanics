[h1]Arms Race Mechanics[/h1]

[b]Why is Germany still on Interwar fighters in 1943?[/b]

You know the problem. It's 1944, you're gearing up for D-Day, and the Axis AI is fielding Interwar-era tanks and '36 fighters. The Soviet Union somehow skipped half its infantry tech. Japan has no carriers. The war feels like a joke because nobody kept up.

This mod fixes that.

[h2]The Short Version[/h2]
ARM doesn't try to make the AI smarter at picking research. Instead, it gives every nation a [b]tech baseline[/b], a floor that makes sure no country is embarrassingly behind in the branches it should be good at. Think of it as the tech a country [i]would obviously have[/i] given its size, industry, and military.

On top of that baseline, normal research still happens. The AI focuses its research slots on what actually matters instead of plugging gaps it shouldn't have in the first place. If you enable it for players, same deal — you get a baseline so you can spend your slots on the research that counts, not catching up on stuff your country should already know.

[b]AI-only by default.[/b] Your research still matters. ARM just makes sure nobody shows up to WW2 with WW1 tech.

[h2]What Changes In Your Game[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]Germany actually has medium tanks.[/b] Major powers won't randomly skip entire branches anymore.
[*] [b]Wars stay hard.[/b] Nations that get invaded don't just roll over with 1936 equipment — they get help catching up.
[*] [b]Minors aren't pushovers.[/b] Romania will have decent infantry tech. Siam will have fighters. They won't rival the USA, but they'll punch back.
[*] [b]Research still matters.[/b] ARM sets the floor, not the ceiling. Your research choices on top of the baseline are what win games.
[/list]

[h2]How Does It Work?[/h2]
Every country gets rated on how powerful, industrial, and militarized it is — think of it like a tier list. Superpowers are at the top, tiny nations are at the bottom.

Then the mod looks at [i]what kind[/i] of country it is. A naval power like Britain or Japan gets naval tech. A land army nation like the USSR gets tanks and artillery. Nobody gets random tech they have no business having.

Throughout the game, countries get baseline tech grants to fill gaps — especially if they're at war and falling behind. This frees up their research slots for the stuff that actually matters, instead of wasting them playing catch-up. It keeps the world competitive without making every country a clone.

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*] 6 power tiers — from tiny nations to superpowers, everyone gets appropriate tech
[*] 4 military branches — Land, Air, Naval, and Industry are tracked separately
[*] War catch-up — countries at war won't fall hopelessly behind in tech
[*] Faction sharing — allies help each other keep up (like real life)
[*] Puppet support — subjects get tech from their overlords
[*] Works with all DLC — automatically adjusts for MtG, NSB, BBA, etc.
[*] 13 game rules — tweak everything from the menu if you want to
[*] AI-only by default — enable it for yourself too to free up your research slots
[/list]
