[h1]Tech Baseline Mechanics[/h1]

Tired of watching Germany roll into 1943 without medium tanks? Or China fighting with 1918 infantry equipment in 1945? TBM fixes that.

[b]Tech Baseline Mechanics[/b] gives every country a believable technology floor based on its actual national power. Majors research what majors should. Minors stay relevant without becoming ahistorical superpowers. Late-game wars are decided by production, planning, and execution — not absurd tech gaps.

[b]Your research is never touched.[/b] TBM targets AI by default. You stay in full control.

[hr][/hr]
[h2]How It Works[/h2]

Every country is scored across five dimensions — economy, science, mobilization, resources, and war posture — and placed into one of [b]six power tiers[/b] (Micro to Superpower). Higher tiers stay closer to the tech frontier. Lower tiers lag behind, but never fall off a cliff.

Four independent [b]branch competence scores[/b] (Land, Air, Naval, Industry) determine which techs a country actually receives. A landlocked nation won't magically get submarine tech. A country with no air industry won't get jets.

Countries at war, in strong alliances, or losing territory get [b]catch-up bonuses[/b] so they don't spiral into permanent irrelevance.

[h2]Features[/h2]

[list]
[*] [b]Six power tiers[/b] with hysteresis — no constant bouncing between tiers
[*] [b]Four branch scores[/b] — Land, Air, Naval, Industry evaluated independently
[*] [b]Smart tech gating[/b] — countries only get tech they can plausibly support
[*] [b]Catch-up mechanics[/b] — war, factions, puppets, and desperation all help
[*] [b]Research speed spirits[/b] — national competence affects your research rate
[*] [b]Historical mode[/b] — gives Germany, UK, Japan, USA, USSR, Italy, France authentic strengths
[*] [b]13 game rules[/b] — customize scope, intensity, tech coverage, doctrines, notifications, and more
[*] [b]Save-safe[/b] — install mid-campaign without breaking your save
[*] [b]Performance-friendly[/b] — staggered evaluation spreads the load across months
[/list]

[hr][/hr]
[h2]Game Rules[/h2]

Everything is configurable from the game rules screen:

[list]
[*] [b]Scope[/b] — AI only, everyone, or disabled
[*] [b]Intensity[/b] — Realistic, Balanced, Arcade, or Historical
[*] [b]Tech scope[/b] — Core only, core + air/naval, all except doctrines, or everything
[*] [b]Doctrines[/b] — Disabled, AI only, or everyone
[*] [b]Advanced tech[/b] — Nuclear/rockets: excluded, superpowers only, or included
[*] [b]Catch-up strength[/b] — War bonuses, faction sharing, puppet tech, anti-death-spiral
[*] [b]Grant caps[/b] — Strict, standard, or unlimited
[*] [b]Bucket spread[/b] — 1/2/3/6 month evaluation cycles (performance tuning)
[*] [b]Notifications[/b] — Silent, major milestones only, or everything
[*] [b]Visibility[/b] — Hide, show own score, or full debug view
[/list]
